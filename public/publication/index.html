January 0001 medRxiv 10.28.20221523(1)
Most scholarly arguments explaining why women discontinue using modern contraceptives whilst in need have focused on sociocultural factors. Drawing on reproductive ecology, we show that a new model taking into account a physiological aetiology is needed to explain interindividual variation in the experience of contraceptive side-effects.
